Before you ask

A little detail saves a lot of back-and-forth.

If you are not sure of the perfect product name, describe the job. If you know the size, thickness, grade, bag count or quantity, include it. If delivery matters, put the postcode and date in the first message.

Clear human context is more helpful than a polished enquiry. A short message from a working buyer is enough when it includes the details that affect price and supply.

What materials can I ask about?

You can request quotes for timber, sheet materials, cement, aggregates and related general building materials.

Can I send a rough list first?

Yes. A rough list is fine if it includes the material category, likely quantity, delivery area and timing.

Do I need exact product codes?

No. Product codes help, but plain descriptions are useful too. Include sizes, grades, thicknesses or intended use where you can.

What should a timber enquiry include?

Include treated or untreated preference, section size, lengths, quantity, grade if known and whether delivery is needed.

What should a sheet material enquiry include?

Include board type, thickness, sheet size, number of sheets and whether the boards are for internal, external or temporary use.

What should I include for cement and aggregates?

Include bag counts, bulk bag needs, aggregate type, site postcode, access notes and the date you need the materials.

Can I use WhatsApp?

Yes. The site includes a WhatsApp link for quick contact. The quote form is still useful because it keeps all request details together.

Quote checklist

Copy this structure into your request if you are in a hurry.

A practical quote request can be only a few lines when the right information is included.

Material

What do you need?

Example: treated timber, 18 mm OSB, cement bags, sharp sand, ballast or mixed site materials.

Quantity

How much is required?

Use sheets, bags, packs, lengths, bulk bags, tonnes or a rough estimate if the final count is not ready.

Logistics

Where and when?

Add postcode, delivery or collection preference, access notes, needed-by date and contact method.

Still unsure?

Use normal site language.

You do not need to write like a catalogue. Write like someone explaining the job to another person: what is being built or repaired, what materials are needed, and what would make the delivery or collection straightforward.

Quick examples

  • Timber: “I need treated timber for framing, sizes are 47 x 100 mm, around 60 lengths.”
  • Sheets: “Please quote 18 mm OSB, 8 x 4 sheets, likely 25 sheets for a refurbishment.”
  • Aggregates: “I need ballast and sharp sand for delivery to a UB postcode next week.”
  • Mixed: “This is for a small job; I need timber, cement and a few sheets together.”
