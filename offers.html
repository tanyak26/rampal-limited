Trade account thinking

Some buyers need one order. Others need a rhythm.

If you buy materials for ongoing work, say so in the request. Repeat needs are easier to understand when the enquiry explains the kind of jobs you handle, the materials you use often and the delivery areas you return to.

The first message can be simple: what you usually buy, roughly how often, and whether the current enquiry is urgent or part of a planned run of work.

Repeat enquiry examples

  • Small contractor: timber, board and cement requests for weekly refurbishment work.
  • Maintenance team: replacement boards, timber lengths and general materials as jobs come in.
  • Groundwork buyer: aggregate and sand needs tied to changing project dates.
  • Purchasing lead: written quote details for internal approval before ordering.
